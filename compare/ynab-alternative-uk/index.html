What is YNAB?

YNAB (You Need A Budget) was founded in 2004 and has become one of the most well-known budgeting apps in the world. Its core philosophy is zero-based budgeting — “give every dollar a job.” Every pound (or dollar) that enters your account is assigned to a specific category before you spend it. It’s a powerful methodology with a devoted community. Both YNAB and earmarkIQ feature in our wider ranking of the best budgeting apps in the UK for 2026.

YNAB offers a 34-day free trial, after which it costs $14.99/month (or $109.99/year). It has a strong web app, iOS app, and Android app, plus extensive educational resources including workshops, a large subreddit (r/ynab), and YouTube tutorials.

However, YNAB was built for the US market. For UK users, this means several significant limitations:

No Open Banking support. YNAB doesn’t connect to UK banks via Open Banking. UK users must manually import transactions via CSV files or use unofficial third-party tools. This is YNAB’s biggest weakness for UK users — it turns a modern budgeting app into a manual spreadsheet exercise.

USD pricing. YNAB charges in US dollars, meaning UK users pay roughly £12/month (varying with exchange rates) plus potential foreign transaction fees on their card.

No UK tax awareness. YNAB has no knowledge of UK tax bands, National Insurance, student loan thresholds, or 2026/27 tax rates. earmarkIQ is built with all of these natively.

No FCA regulation. YNAB is a US company with no FCA oversight. earmarkIQ is an FCA Appointed Representative of Finexer Ltd (FRN 925695).
