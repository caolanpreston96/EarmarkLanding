Quick Summary

Plum and earmarkIQ are both UK fintech apps that connect to your bank, but they solve very different problems. Both also feature in our wider ranking of the best budgeting apps in the UK for 2026.

Plum is primarily an automated savings app. It analyses your spending patterns, calculates what you can afford to save, and automatically moves that money into a savings pot. It also offers Cash ISAs, stocks & shares ISAs, and interest on balances. Plum is excellent at building the savings habit — but it’s not really a budgeting app. Its spending analytics are basic, it has no subscription tracking, no price creep detection, and no salary allocation.

earmarkIQ is a complete financial operating system. It matches Plum’s goal of helping you save, then adds AI salary allocation (splitting your pay before you spend it), detailed spending analysis, subscription price creep detection, a full AI financial advisor (Ask IQ) with transaction context, partner finance mode, goal tracking, net worth tracking, and 10 pro financial planning tools. Where Plum automates one piece of the puzzle, earmarkIQ manages the whole picture.

Where Plum Genuinely Wins

Automated saving is genuinely clever. Plum’s core feature analyses your spending patterns and automatically moves affordable amounts into savings. It’s a set-and-forget approach that works particularly well for people who struggle to save consistently. The algorithm gets smarter over time and adjusts to your spending habits.

Investment options built in. Plum offers stocks & shares ISAs, Cash ISAs, and interest on balances directly within the app. You can invest spare change into diversified funds without leaving the app. earmarkIQ’s marketplace recommends financial products but doesn’t hold your money or offer ISA wrappers directly.

Available now with a proven track record. Plum is live, well-established, and has millions of users in the UK. earmarkIQ launches mid-2026. If you need automated saving right now, Plum is a proven choice while you wait.

Lower entry point. Plum Pro at £2.99/mo is cheaper than earmarkIQ Premium at £4.99/mo. If you only need the savings automation and nothing else, Plum’s paid tiers start lower.

Where earmarkIQ Wins

Salary allocation. earmarkIQ’s core feature — AI splits your salary into bills, savings, investments, and guilt-free spending on payday. Plum has no salary allocation feature at all. It saves money after the fact; earmarkIQ plans before you spend.

Subscription tracking & price creep detection. earmarkIQ tracks every recurring payment and alerts you the moment any subscription increases its price, showing old price, new price, and annual impact. Plum has no subscription tracking at all — you won’t know your Netflix went up until you check your bank statement. We explain how this compounds in our UK subscription tracking guide.

Ask IQ. earmarkIQ’s AI advisor (powered by Claude from Anthropic) has full context of your transactions, bills, and goals. Ask “Can I afford a holiday in June?” and get a specific, data-backed answer. Plum has no AI advisor or chat feature.

Partner finance mode. earmarkIQ is the only UK budgeting app with a proper partner finance feature — combined household view, shared goals, split bills — while keeping personal accounts private. Plum is strictly a single-user app. See our guide to managing money as a couple.

Pro financial tools. earmarkIQ includes 10 planning tools — debt payoff planner, tax optimiser, salary raise calculator, what-if simulator, investment calculator, and more — that Plum doesn’t offer.

Spending analysis is far more detailed. earmarkIQ provides detailed category breakdowns, payday spike detection, a 7-type anomaly scanner, weekly AI briefings, and historical spending trends. Plum’s spending analytics are basic at best — it’s fundamentally a savings app, not a spending tracker.

Budgeting features. Plum is primarily a savings vehicle — it doesn’t help you plan how to spend your money, just how to save some of it. earmarkIQ gives you a complete picture: where your money goes, where it should go, and how to optimise the difference.
