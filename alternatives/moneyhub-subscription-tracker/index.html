What Moneyhub Offered

Moneyhub’s subscription tracker automatically detected recurring payments from your connected bank accounts. It showed you a list of active subscriptions, their amounts, and payment dates. It was useful but relatively basic — no price change alerts, no categorisation beyond “subscriptions,” and no insight into whether you were overpaying.

earmarkIQ’s Subscription Radar

earmarkIQ’s Subscription Radar is a complete upgrade over Moneyhub’s subscription tracking. It automatically detects all recurring payments via Open Banking and categorises them into three types:

Bills — Fixed commitments like rent, council tax, and utilities (£80/mo average).
Subscriptions — Services like Netflix, Spotify, gym memberships (£79.73/mo average).
Daily Habits — Recurring micro-transactions like coffee, meal deals, commuting (£131.99/mo average).

The total average across all three categories is £291.72/mo — and most users don’t realise the full extent until they see the breakdown.

✦ Price Creep Detection

earmarkIQ’s standout feature is price creep detection. When any subscription silently increases its price, earmarkIQ alerts you immediately — showing the old price, new price, and the annual cost impact. On average, users discover +£45/year in hidden price increases across just 5 subscriptions.
