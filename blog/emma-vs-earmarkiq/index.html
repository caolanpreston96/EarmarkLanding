Quick Summary

Emma and earmarkIQ are both UK-native Open Banking apps that connect to your bank accounts and help you manage your money. That's where the similarity largely ends. Both also feature in our wider ranking of the best budgeting apps in the UK for 2026, where we place them in the context of Snoop, Plum, Monzo, YNAB and PocketSmith.

Emma is a mature, well-designed app focused on spending categorisation and subscription tracking. It shows you what you've spent and where. It's available now, has a large user community, and its free tier is genuinely usable. The main limitation is that Emma is a retrospective tool — it's excellent at showing you the past but offers limited guidance on what to do next.

earmarkIQ is an AI-native app built around salary allocation — telling you how to split your money before you spend it, not just analysing what you spent after. It matches Emma on spending categorisation and subscription tracking, and adds significant capabilities Emma doesn't have: proactive price creep detection, an AI advisor with full transaction context, partner finance mode, and 10 pro financial planning tools. It's launching mid-2026.
